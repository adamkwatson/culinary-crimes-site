The Manuka Honey Food Crime Case in Netherlands

Category: Food Fraud | Country: Netherlands | Crime Type: Counterfeit Ingredient Scheme

The Problem

Manuka Honey has repeatedly appeared in food fraud investigations involving counterfeit or adulterated products. In Netherlands, regulators discovered shipments labeled as premium Manuka Honey that were diluted or substituted with cheaper ingredients. Such schemes allow dishonest suppliers to increase profits while deceiving consumers. Agencies including the FDA’s Office of Criminal Investigations have pursued cases involving distributors who knowingly sold fraudulent Manuka Honey, highlighting the ongoing challenges in protecting the integrity of the global food supply.

Interesting Fact

Experts consider this product highly vulnerable to fraud due to its market value.
